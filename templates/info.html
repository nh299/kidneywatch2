Diabetes

An effective diabetes diet will look like a healthy diet for anyone. Limiting salt and sodium is important for those with diabetes and chronic kidney disease (CKD). In many cases reducing potassium, phosphorus, and protein is needed. To learn more about diabetes and CKD, visit these resources.


  • CDC - Diabetes & Kidney Disease
  • Alberta Health Services - Healthy Eating For Diabetes

    • High Blood Pressure

    Eating a healthy diet will lower your blood pressure. An effective diet for individuals with high blood pressure and CKD is low in fat and cholesterol. Limiting foods that are high in saturated fat as well as full-fat dairy foods will help with hypertension. Eating fresh fruit and vegetables and whole grains will promote heart health. To learn more about high blood pressure and CKD, visit these resources.


  • NIH - High Blood Pressure & Kidney Disease
  • National Kidney Foundation - The DASH Diet

    • Obesity

    Being overwight has a direct, and negative effect on your kidneys. A high body mass index is one of the strongest risk factors for developing CKD. An effective diet will focus on avoiding processed foods and meals with large amounts of salt and sugar. Focusing on eating lean-proteins and excersicing every day will also dramatically help with obesity. To learn more about obesity and CKD, visit these resources.


  • American Kidney Fund - Obesity & Chronic Kidney Disease
  • Fresenius Kidney Care - The Basics of Losing Weight with Kidney Disease
